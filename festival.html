GMA's Festivals!

Overview of General Mariano Alvarez (GMA) Location and History: General Mariano Alvarez (GMA) is the youngest town in the Province of Cavite. It was formerly a barangay of the Municipality of Carmona. The town is named after General Mariano Alvarez, a revolutionary general from Noveleta. Founding and Celebrations: GMA celebrated its 44th Founding Anniversary on March 14, 2025, marking the day it was officially classified as a municipality in 1981. This is also celebrated alongside its 17th Kabuteño Festival.
The Kabuteño Festival was established in 2005 to promote the OTOP, and it has grown into an annual event celebrating the Cultured Oyster Mushroom. In its 16th year, the festival continues to highlight the production, nutritional value, and marketability of the mushroom, further benefiting local producers and the municipality.

Economic Values

Economic Focus Mushroom Culture: GMA is known as the Mushroom Capital of Cavite due to its well-developed mushroom culture facilities. The town’s economy is focused on boosting local growth while ensuring universal accessibility for all residents.

Religious Mentions

General Mariano Alvarez in Cavite is a hidden gem that beautifully blends urban development with a sense of community and tradition. The town boasts a serene, rural charm, with lush landscapes and tree-lined streets that provide a refreshing contrast to the bustling cities nearby. Its vibrant local culture is reflected in the warm, welcoming spirit of its people, who take pride in their heritage and close-knit neighborhoods. The religious sites, such as the Saint Joseph the Worker Parish and various Christian churches, add a peaceful, spiritual essence to the town’s atmosphere. Whether it’s strolling through the lively barangays or admiring the scenic views, GMA offers a unique blend of tranquility and cultural richness, making it an ideal place to experience both natural beauty and a strong sense of community.
