Constellations of GMA

The Life of General Mariano Alvarez

General Mariano Alvarez was a prominent figure during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. Born on August 22, 1869, in Binakayan, Cavite, Alvarez was an active participant in the revolutionary struggle. He played a key role in the fight for independence as a member of the Katipunan, the revolutionary society that aimed to overthrow Spanish rule. General Alvarez was a trusted leader and became known for his courage and strategic acumen. He rose to prominence as a commander in the Philippine Revolutionary Army, fighting alongside other revolutionary leaders such as Emilio Aguinaldo. Alvarez’s efforts contributed to the eventual success of the Philippine Revolution, and he continued to serve the country during the Philippine-American War. He was recognized for his leadership, integrity, and commitment to the cause of independence, earning a place in history as one of the most respected revolutionary figures from Cavite.

The Early Development of General Mariano Alvarez (GMA), Cavite

The municipality of General Mariano Alvarez (GMA), located in the province of Cavite, has a rich and fascinating history that dates back to the 1960s. Originally part of the municipality of Carmona, GMA was once a resettlement area for families relocated from Quezon Memorial Park in Diliman, Quezon City. The development of the area began in March 1968, with the help of various government agencies and private organizations. The People's Homesite and Housing Corporation (PHHC) prepared the basic development concepts, while the National Planning Commission (NPC) and the Department of Health (DOH) provided support. The 51st Engineering Brigade of the 514th Engineering Battalion of the Philippine Army built the access road and internal roads within the project.

Infrastructure and Community Growth

As the area grew, infrastructure such as schools, health facilities, and waterworks systems were built. The National Waterworks and Sewerage Administration (NWSA) drilled wells, and the Department of Education established schools, including San Gabriel I Elementary School, San Gabriel II Elementary School, and Carmona Relocation Center High School.

Establishment of GMA as a Municipality

In 1977 and 1978, the Sangguniang Bayan of Carmona endorsed resolutions to convert the area into a new municipality. The bill creating the municipality was authored by Assemblywoman Helena Z. Benitez and Minister Jose A. Roño. A plebiscite was held on June 13, 1980, and the majority voted in favor of creating the new municipality, which was named in honor of General Mariano Alvarez, recognizing his significant role in the fight for independence and his legacy in Cavite.

The Inauguration of GMA

The first set of municipal officials was appointed, with Leoniso G. Virata as the first mayor. The municipality was officially inaugurated on March 14, 1981. Initially, it comprised 10 barangays, which were later divided into 27 barangays, all named after revolutionary leaders and followers of General Mariano Alvarez.

Present Day

Today, General Mariano Alvarez is a thriving and developed community, thanks to the efforts of its municipal officials and leaders. The municipality continues to grow and progress, with a strong focus on providing quality services to its residents and promoting sustainable development.
